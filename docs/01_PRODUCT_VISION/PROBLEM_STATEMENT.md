# Problem Statement

Version: 1.0  
Project: STEG Smart Internship & Administrative Workflow Platform

---

# 1. Purpose

This document describes the current problems and limitations associated with the internship management process at STEG.

The objective is to clearly identify the operational challenges that justify the creation of the STEG Smart Internship & Administrative Workflow Platform.

Understanding these problems is essential to ensure that every technical and functional decision made during the project directly addresses a real organizational need.

---

# 2. Current Situation Overview

Currently, internship management involves multiple stakeholders, departments, documents, and validation steps.

The process requires coordination between:

- Internship candidates.
- Human Resources department.
- Internship supervisors.
- Financial department.
- Administrative managers.
- Directors.

However, many activities are still handled through traditional manual methods, creating difficulties in tracking, communication, and process optimization.

The absence of a unified digital platform results in fragmented information, repetitive tasks, and limited visibility over internship activities.

---

# 3. Main Problem Statement

STEG currently lacks a centralized digital system capable of managing the complete internship lifecycle from application submission to internship completion.

The absence of such a platform creates several operational challenges:

- Manual management of internship requests.
- Physical document circulation.
- Difficulty tracking application progress.
- Lack of centralized internship information.
- Limited workflow automation.
- Weak visibility for decision makers.
- Difficult historical data consultation.
- Increased administrative workload.

Therefore, there is a need for a secure, scalable, and intelligent platform capable of digitalizing and optimizing internship management processes.

---

# 4. Identified Problems

---

# Problem 01 — Paper-Based Administrative Processes

## Description

A significant part of internship management relies on physical documents and manual exchanges.

Examples include:

- Internship applications.
- University documents.
- Internship conventions.
- Assignment letters.
- Certificates.
- Administrative forms.

---

## Consequences

This creates:

- Slow document processing.
- Risk of document loss.
- Difficulty searching historical records.
- Increased paper consumption.
- Additional administrative effort.

---

## Required Improvement

Introduce a digital document management system with:

- Secure storage.
- Document versioning.
- Automatic generation.
- Online submission.
- Easy retrieval.

---

# Problem 02 — Lack of Centralized Information

## Description

Internship information is distributed between different actors and departments.

Examples:

- Candidate information.
- Internship details.
- Supervisor assignments.
- Documents.
- Payments.
- Evaluations.

---

## Consequences

This leads to:

- Duplicate information.
- Data inconsistency.
- Difficult information retrieval.
- Poor global visibility.

---

## Required Improvement

Create a single source of truth where all internship-related information is centralized and synchronized.

---

# Problem 03 — Manual Workflow Management

## Description

Validation and approval processes often require manual coordination between departments.

Examples:

- Application review.
- Internship approval.
- Document validation.
- Payment approval.

---

## Consequences

This causes:

- Slow decision cycles.
- Lack of process visibility.
- Difficulty knowing pending actions.
- Missing accountability.

---

## Required Improvement

Implement a configurable workflow engine supporting:

- Approval steps.
- Validation actions.
- Status tracking.
- Workflow history.
- Automatic notifications.

---

# Problem 04 — Limited Candidate Experience

## Description

Candidates have limited visibility during the internship application process.

They may not know:

- Whether their application was received.
- Current application status.
- Required next actions.
- Final decision.

---

## Consequences

This creates:

- Communication delays.
- Repeated requests for information.
- Poor user experience.

---

## Required Improvement

Provide a digital candidate portal allowing:

- Online application.
- Document upload.
- Status tracking.
- Notifications.

---

# Problem 05 — Difficult Internship Monitoring

## Description

During the internship period, monitoring intern activities requires manual follow-up.

Supervisors may lack:

- Centralized internship progress information.
- Structured journal tracking.
- Digital deliverable management.
- Evaluation history.

---

## Consequences

This results in:

- Reduced visibility on intern progress.
- Difficult evaluation process.
- Additional communication effort.

---

## Required Improvement

Provide an internship companion application supporting:

- Daily journal.
- Tasks.
- Deliverables.
- Supervisor validation.
- Feedback.

---

# Problem 06 — Weak Communication Between Stakeholders

## Description

Communication between candidates, interns, supervisors, HR, and other departments is often performed through disconnected channels.

Examples:

- Emails.
- Phone calls.
- Physical exchanges.
- Informal messages.

---

## Consequences

This creates:

- Information delays.
- Missing communication history.
- Difficult follow-up.

---

## Required Improvement

Introduce centralized communication mechanisms:

- Notifications.
- Alerts.
- Workflow messages.
- Activity history.

---

# Problem 07 — Lack of Real-Time Management Visibility

## Description

Decision makers do not always have immediate access to global internship information.

Examples:

- Number of active internships.
- Internship distribution by department.
- Supervisor workload.
- Historical statistics.

---

## Consequences

This limits:

- Strategic analysis.
- Resource planning.
- Performance monitoring.

---

## Required Improvement

Provide dashboards and reporting capabilities based on centralized data.

---

# Problem 08 — Security and Traceability Challenges

## Description

Administrative processes require protection of sensitive information.

Examples:

- Candidate personal data.
- Official documents.
- Financial information.
- Administrative decisions.

---

## Consequences

Without proper controls:

- Unauthorized access may occur.
- Actions cannot always be traced.
- Data governance becomes difficult.

---

## Required Improvement

Implement enterprise security mechanisms:

- Authentication.
- Authorization.
- Role-based permissions.
- Audit logs.
- Secure document storage.

---

# 5. Root Causes Analysis

| Root Cause | Resulting Problems |
|------------|-------------------|
| Lack of centralized platform | Information fragmentation |
| Manual processes | Slow execution and errors |
| Paper dependency | Document loss and difficult retrieval |
| No workflow automation | Delayed approvals |
| Disconnected communication channels | Poor collaboration |
| Limited reporting capabilities | Weak decision support |
| Absence of structured monitoring | Difficult internship evaluation |

Current limitations increase:

- Processing time.

- Human errors.
- Follow-up complexity.

---

## User Impact

Different stakeholders experience:

- Reduced transparency.
- Communication difficulties.
- Limited access to information.

---

## Organizational Impact

---

# 7. Need for a Digital Solution

The identified problems demonstrate the necessity of implementing a unified digital platform capable of:

- Managing the complete internship lifecycle.
- Automating administrative workflows.
- Centralizing information.
- Improving collaboration.
- Securing sensitive data.
- Providing real-time visibility.
- Supporting future digital transformation.

---

# 8. Problem Statement Summary

STEG needs a modern internship management platform because current manual and fragmented processes limit efficiency, transparency, and scalability.

The STEG Smart Internship & Administrative Workflow Platform addresses these challenges by introducing a secure, automated, and scalable digital ecosystem that connects all stakeholders and transforms internship management into a structured and intelligent process.
