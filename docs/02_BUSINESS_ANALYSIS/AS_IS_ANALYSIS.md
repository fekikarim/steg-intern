# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Purpose of This Document

This document describes the **current state (AS-IS)** of the internship management process within STEG before the implementation of the Smart Internship & Administrative Workflow Platform.

The objective is to understand:

- How internship requests are currently handled.
- Which actors are involved.
- Which documents and information flows exist.
- Existing manual operations.
- Current limitations and inefficiencies.
- Opportunities for digital transformation.

This analysis represents the starting point for defining the future optimized process (**TO-BE Analysis**) enabled by the proposed digital platform.

---

# 2. Current Internship Management Overview

Currently, internship management inside STEG relies mainly on:

- Paper-based administrative procedures.
- Manual document exchange.
- Physical validation signatures.
- Email communication.
- Separate information storage between departments.
- Human follow-up of internship progress.

The process involves multiple stakeholders:

- Students / Internship candidates.
- Human Resources department.
- Internship supervisors.
- Administrative agents.
- Financial department.
- Management.

However, there is no unified digital platform connecting all these actors.

# 4. Internship Application Process

## 4.1 Current Workflow

A student wishing to complete an internship at STEG generally follows these steps:

1. Student prepares internship request documents.
2. Documents are submitted physically or through email.
3. Administrative staff receive and review documents.
4. Required information is checked manually.
5. Internship request is forwarded for validation.
6. Decision is communicated manually.

---

## 4.2 Current Actors Involved

| Actor | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Candidate / Student | Prepare and submit internship request |
| Administrative Agent | Receive and verify documents |
| HR Department | Manage internship requests and decisions |
| Department Manager | Validate internship placement |
| Supervisor | Accept and supervise intern |

---

# 5. Document Management (Current State)

## 5.1 Existing Documents

Internship management requires multiple documents:

- Internship request letter.
- CV.
- Motivation letter.
- University convention.
- Identity documents.
- Internship agreement.
- Assignment documents.
- Internship certificate.
- Evaluation documents.

---

## 5.2 Current Document Handling

Documents are currently managed through:

- Physical folders.
- Shared directories.
- Emails.
- Manual archives.

There is no centralized document repository with:

- Version management.
- Automatic classification.
- Search capabilities.
- Access control.
- Document history.

---

# 6. Internship Assignment Process

## Current Workflow

After validation:

1. Candidate is assigned to a department.
2. A supervisor is selected.
3. Assignment information is recorded manually.
4. Student receives internship information.
5. Supervisor starts internship follow-up.

---

## Current Limitations

The assignment process depends heavily on human coordination:

- No automatic supervisor availability checking.
- No centralized assignment history.
- Difficult tracking of previous assignments.
- Limited visibility for management.

---

# 7. Internship Follow-Up Process

## Current Situation

During the internship period:

- Communication happens mainly through direct contact.
- Progress tracking depends on the supervisor.
- Internship activities are not always formally documented.
- Deliverables are exchanged manually.

---

## Current Follow-Up Methods

Examples:

- Emails.
- Paper reports.
- Physical meetings.
- Personal communication tools.

---

## Missing Capabilities

Current process does not provide:

- Digital internship journal.
- Task management.
- Online deliverable submission.
- Real-time progress tracking.
- Automatic notifications.

---

# 8. Evaluation Process (AS-IS)

## Current Workflow

At the end of the internship:

1. Supervisor evaluates intern performance.
2. Evaluation documents are prepared manually.
3. Results are archived.

---

## Current Problems

- Evaluation criteria may vary between supervisors.
- No standardized evaluation framework.
- Historical evaluations are difficult to analyze.
- No performance analytics.

---

# 9. Financial Management Process

## Current Situation

Internship indemnities and payments are handled separately.

Process generally includes:

1. Verification of internship eligibility.
2. Calculation of payment amount.
3. Approval process.
4. Payment execution.
5. Archiving of financial documents.

---

## Current Limitations

- Manual tracking.
- Risk of data inconsistency.
- Difficult payment history consultation.
- Limited integration with internship information.

---

# 10. Communication Process

## Current Communication Channels

Communication currently uses:

- Email.
- Phone calls.
- Physical meetings.
- Internal messaging.

---

## Current Problems

There is no centralized notification system for:

- Application status updates.
- Validation requests.
- Internship reminders.
- Task deadlines.
- Document notifications.

# 12. Current Technical Environment

Existing internship management does not currently provide a dedicated integrated platform.

Characteristics:

- Multiple independent tools.
- Limited interoperability.
- No unified backend.
- No mobile application.
- No workflow automation engine.

# 14. Root Causes Analysis

## Organizational Causes

- Historical dependence on manual procedures.
- Multiple departments involved without centralized coordination.
- Lack of unified digital workflow.

---

## Technical Causes

- Absence of dedicated internship management software.
- Lack of integrated databases.
- No automated workflow engine.

---

## Operational Causes

- High administrative workload.
- Repetitive manual tasks.
- Difficult information retrieval.

---

# 15. Conclusion

The current internship management process at STEG is functional but highly dependent on manual operations.

The absence of a centralized digital ecosystem generates:

- Administrative delays.
- Increased workload.
- Limited visibility.
- Difficult tracking.
- Reduced efficiency.

The analysis of the AS-IS situation demonstrates the necessity of implementing a modern platform capable of:

- Digitizing internship workflows.
- Centralizing information.
- Automating administrative operations.
- Improving communication.
- Providing traceability and analytics.

The next step is defining the optimized future process through the **TO-BE Analysis**, describing how the STEG Smart Internship Platform will transform these operations.
